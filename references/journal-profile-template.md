## Logic Notes By Journal Type

### Broad high-impact journals

Prioritize a clear problem-to-discovery arc, strong but supportable novelty, and broad significance. Do not upgrade association into mechanism or preliminary evidence into field-changing conclusion.

### Specialist journals

Prioritize technical precision, complete methods, exact results, and field-specific terminology. Novelty may be narrower, but claims must be anchored in the study design.

### Clinical journals

Prioritize population, intervention/exposure, comparator, outcome, study design, effect size, uncertainty, safety, and clinical relevance. Do not use clinical utility, efficacy, safety, or recommendation language unless the study design supports it.

### Methods journals

Prioritize reproducibility, benchmark design, validation dataset, comparator methods, performance metrics, limitations, and availability. Avoid saying a method is universal unless tested across adequate settings.

### Review journals

Prioritize scope, selection logic, synthesis, balance, and citation support. Do not present review-level interpretations as original data.

## Profile Quality Check

Before translating, confirm:

- The selected article type matches the user's text.
- The abstract and section structure match the journal.
- Required reporting checklist issues are known.
- The profile does not impose a stronger story than the manuscript supports.
