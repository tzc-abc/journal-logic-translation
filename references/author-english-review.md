# Author English Review

Use this when the user provides their own English translation and asks whether it is good, natural, publishable, or suitable for a target journal.

## Review Posture

Preserve good author wording. Do not replace a strong user translation with a full model rewrite just because another phrasing is possible.

Prioritize:

1. Scientific accuracy and evidence boundaries.
2. Target-journal fit.
3. Technical clarity.
4. Terminology consistency.
5. Minimal polishing.

## Decision Levels

- `Ready to use`: only optional micro-edits are needed.
- `Usable with light edits`: grammar, flow, terminology, or style needs small changes.
- `Needs revision`: claim strength, logic, missing definitions, or unsupported wording may affect review.
- `Must clarify before use`: the English depends on missing data, statistics, methods, or journal requirements.

## Common Micro-Edits

- Prefer `15.0-fold` or `a 15.0-fold increase` over `15.0 times` in formal results writing when describing fold change.
- Use `measured in`, `obtained from`, or `derived from` to clarify where a ratio, variable, or response comes from.
- Keep terminology stable: if the manuscript treats CRSS as a defined variable, decide whether to use `CRSS` or `the CRSS` and apply it consistently.
- Use `whereas` for technical contrasts when both clauses are parallel.
- Use serial commas in formal lists for clarity.
- Keep hyphenation consistent: `near-contact region`, `particle-damping experiments`, `energy-type response`.
- Preserve cautious verbs such as `indicates`, `suggests`, `is associated with`, and `should be interpreted as` when evidence is from parameter sweeps, simulations, or correlations.

## Engineering Journal Preference

For engineering journals, favor direct technical prose:

- State what the parameter sweep, model, or experiment shows.
- Preserve numerical comparisons and units.
- Keep interpretation close to the result.
- Avoid ornate novelty claims.
- Use boundary sentences when model-scale evidence should not be equated with macroscopic experiments.

## Review Output

Use `references/output-patterns.md` Author English Review Mode. If the text is already strong, say so first, then provide a polished version only if edits improve precision or consistency.

## Do Not

- Do not add new mechanisms, citations, statistics, or validation claims.
- Do not over-polish into vague prose.
- Do not remove an author's useful boundary statement.
- Do not store the user's manuscript text as an example unless explicitly approved and anonymized.
